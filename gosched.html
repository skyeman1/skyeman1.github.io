My last public show at Griffith Observatory using the old format show --
where we had a specific topic for a few months and could easily add pertinent,
current and correct information to a show "on the fly", was on December 30, 2001 at 7:30 pm:
#4679 of a series starting on February 12, 1976.
That last old-format show was "The Christmas Star."

My first public show after becoming a lecturer was a very crowded (about 460 of 635 seats)
Friday night show on February 29, 1976: about Flying Saucers and UFOs.

Lecturers started doing similar current events-based shows
with more audience interaction during the renovation
at the Griffith Observatory Satellite facility near the L.A.,
from our 69th anniversary (May 14, 2003) to September 3, 2006.
(My last shows at The Satellite were 9/2/06.)

 
So, if you like statistics, I've been working at Griffith
for 72.24% of my life, including during the renovation and pandemic closure.
That’s 56.60% of the time since Griffith first opened on May 14, 1935!

(I think that the only person who's worked there longer than I have is Dr. Krupp.)

For 94.61% of the time I've worked at Griffith, I've been a Lecturer.
That's 68.35% of my life: well over 2/3rds!
This is over 9000 shows to perhaps as many as 1.3 million people.

February "29", 2023 will mark the completion of my 47th year as a lecturer.
(My actual change-in-status from Guide to Lecturer is recorded as 2/29/76 on official City records,
but I was hired after a public show on 2/12/76.)

June 10, 2022 completed my 49th year of being employed part-time at Griffith Observatory,
starting as a guide in June 1973!
